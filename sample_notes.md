# Biology 101
Tags: Science, Biology, Intro
Description: An introductory course covering the fundamentals of biology.

## Cell Structure
Summary: Overview of eukaryotic and prokaryotic cells.

All living organisms are made of cells. There are two main types:

**Prokaryotic cells** – no membrane-bound nucleus (e.g. bacteria).
**Eukaryotic cells** – have a nucleus and organelles (e.g. animal and plant cells).

Key organelles:
- Nucleus: contains DNA
- Mitochondria: powerhouse of the cell (ATP production)
- Ribosomes: protein synthesis
- Cell membrane: controls what enters/exits

## Photosynthesis
Summary: How plants convert sunlight into energy.

Photosynthesis occurs in the chloroplasts of plant cells.

Equation: 6CO2 + 6H2O + light energy → C6H12O6 + 6O2

Stages:
1. Light-dependent reactions (in the thylakoid membrane)
2. Calvin cycle / light-independent reactions (in the stroma)

## Genetics Basics
Summary: Introduction to DNA, genes, and heredity.

DNA (deoxyribonucleic acid) carries genetic information.
- Made of nucleotides: Adenine, Thymine, Guanine, Cytosine
- Double helix structure discovered by Watson & Crick (1953)

Mendel's Laws:
- Law of Segregation
- Law of Independent Assortment
